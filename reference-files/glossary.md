# Glossary

## Archive

The only permitted form of "deletion" for core entities (Wallets, Categories, Sources). Archived entities are hidden from active UI but retained in full for historical integrity. Archived entities remain editable.

## Batch Operation

Functionality that allows multiple transactions to be added or edited in a single action.

## Bill

An entity representing a payable obligation tied to a Source. Referenced by a Transaction as the `to` field when the obligation is settled. Can be recurring.

## Category

A classification label applied to transactions for grouping and reporting purposes. Non-deletable; archived only.

## External

Represents any entity outside the system boundary — not a Wallet, Source, or any other internal entity. Used as `from` when money enters the system, and as `to` when money leaves it.

## Internal Transfer

A transaction where both `from` and `to` are Wallets within the system.

## Manual Adjustment

A system-generated transaction used to reconcile a Wallet's balance when funds cannot be tracked. Both `from` and `to` point to the same Wallet.

## Recurring Item

A Bill or Revenue configured to repeat on a schedule. Scheduling logic must account for variable month lengths (28, 29, 30, or 31 days).

## Revenue

An entity representing money generated by or attributed to a Source. Referenced by a Transaction as the `from` field when the revenue is realized. Can be recurring.

## Source

The underlying reason behind a Bill or Revenue — not the counterparty of the transaction. A single Source can originate both Bills and Revenues. Not directly part of a Transaction; it attaches to Bill and Revenue entities. Non-deletable; archived only.

> **Example:** A plumber repairing an apartment is the service provider, but the Source of the bill is the Apartment.

## Transaction

The fundamental unit of money movement in the system. Defined by a `from` and a `to` field. Every monetary movement — digital or physical — must be recorded as a Transaction.

The analytical meaning of a transaction is derived from its `from`/`to` combination:

| `from`        | `to`          | Represents            |
| :------------ | :------------ | :-------------------- |
| External      | Wallet        | Money entering system |
| Wallet        | External      | Money leaving system  |
| Revenue       | Wallet        | Revenue realized      |
| Wallet        | Bill          | Bill paid             |
| Wallet        | Wallet        | Internal Transfer     |
| Wallet (same) | Wallet (same) | Manual Adjustment     |

## Wallet

An internal location where money is held. Represents both digital accounts and physical cash. Non-deletable; archived only.

> **Examples:** Inter Current Account, Inter Safes, Physical Cash.
